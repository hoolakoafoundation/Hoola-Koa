Why this matters

For too many men in Hawaiʻi, the first sign is a heart attack.

1 in 2

U.S. adults has high blood pressure.

1 in 6

don't know they have it — it has no symptoms.

33%

less likely men are to see a doctor than women.

1.5×

the rate Native Hawaiian & Pacific Islander adults die from heart disease.

1 in 3

cardiac events happen before age 65.

80%

of heart attacks & strokes are preventable.

It's called the silent killer for a reason. You can't feel high blood pressure.
— American Heart Association

You feel fine. You're working, providing, showing up for your family. So a doctor's visit drops to the bottom of the list — and high blood pressure builds quietly for years.

The only way to know is to check. A screening takes about two minutes, costs nothing, and tells you exactly where you stand. If your numbers are high, we help you take the next step — no insurance required.

Hoʻōla Koa means healing strength. Koa is the warrior — the strong one.

What we offer

Simple, free, and built for our men.

We meet you where you already are — the barbershop, the job site, the community gathering — and make taking care of yourself the easy choice.

Free BP Screening

A trained screener checks your blood pressure on the spot. Takes about two minutes — no appointment, no paperwork hassle, no cost.

Know Your Numbers

We explain what your reading actually means in plain language — so you walk away knowing whether you're in the clear or need to act.

Health Navigation

If your numbers are high, we connect you one-on-one to a community health center or doctor near you — even without insurance.

How it works

Three steps. About two minutes.

No clinic. No waiting room. No catch.

3

Get connected

Need follow-up? We point you to free or low-cost care nearby — and help you take the next step.
